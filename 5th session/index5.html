Title Short Name About
White Hat hacker W.H.H. A white hat hacker is an ethical cybersecurity expert who uses their skills to identify and fix vulnerabilities in systems, networks, or applications, aiming to improve overall security and protect against malicious attacks. They work within legal boundaries and often collaborate with organizations to strengthen defenses and mitigate risks.
Black Hat hacker B.H.H. A black hat hacker is a cybercriminal who exploits vulnerabilities in systems, networks, or applications for malicious purposes, such as theft, data breaches, or disruption. Their activities are illegal and unethical, often causing significant harm to individuals, organizations, and infrastructure.
Grey Hat hacker G.H.H. A grey hat hacker straddles the line between ethical and malicious hacking, often discovering and exploiting vulnerabilities without permission but without malicious intent. They may report these vulnerabilities to the affected organizations, sometimes seeking compensation, but their actions are typically unauthorized and can still be illegal.
